Access monitor provides visibility into how your OSS resources are accessed. Use it to audit access patterns, verify that access policies work as intended, and identify unused or infrequently accessed objects.

## Use cases

- Verify that block-public-access settings are effective.
- Identify objects accessed from unexpected regions or IP ranges.
- Detect stale data that can be transitioned to lower-cost storage classes.

## Enable Access monitor

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable at the bucket level">
    Navigate to your bucket, choose **Data Monitoring > Access Monitor**, and enable the feature.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review access reports">
    Access monitor generates reports showing access frequency, source locations, and access methods for objects in your bucket.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Integration with other services

| Service | Integration |
|---------|------------|
| CloudMonitor | Metrics feed into CloudMonitor for threshold-based alerting |
| ActionTrail | Tracks management operations (API calls, console actions) for compliance auditing |
| Cloud Config | Evaluates bucket configurations against compliance rules |

<Note>
ActionTrail captures management-plane operations (e.g., changing bucket settings), while access logging and access monitor capture data-plane operations (e.g., GetObject, PutObject).
</Note>
