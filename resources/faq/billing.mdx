---
title: 'Billing FAQ'
description: 'Frequently asked questions about OSS pricing and billing'
---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What am I charged for?">
    OSS charges for:
    - **Storage**: Based on the amount of data stored, billed per GB/month
    - **Traffic**: Outbound internet traffic (inbound is free)
    - **API requests**: Per 10,000 PUT/GET/LIST requests
    - **Data retrieval**: For IA, Archive, Cold Archive, and Deep Cold Archive
    - **Data processing**: Image processing, video snapshots, etc.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is inbound (upload) traffic free?">
    Yes. Data uploaded to OSS (inbound traffic) is free. You only pay for outbound internet traffic when downloading data.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is same-region ECS to OSS traffic free?">
    Yes. Traffic between ECS and OSS in the same region via internal endpoints is free. Use the internal endpoint (e.g., `oss-cn-hangzhou-internal.aliyuncs.com`) to avoid traffic charges.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the minimum storage duration?">
    Objects in IA, Archive, Cold Archive, and Deep Cold Archive have minimum storage durations. If deleted before the minimum period, you are charged for the remaining time:
    - IA: 30 days
    - Archive: 60 days
    - Cold Archive: 180 days
    - Deep Cold Archive: 180 days
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How can I reduce costs?">
    - Use lifecycle rules to transition infrequently accessed data to cheaper storage classes
    - Purchase Storage Capacity Units (SCUs) for predictable workloads (up to 50%+ discount)
    - Use CDN for frequent downloads (CDN traffic is cheaper than direct OSS traffic)
    - Use internal endpoints from same-region ECS
    - Clean up incomplete multipart uploads with lifecycle rules
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What are Storage Capacity Units (SCUs)?">
    SCUs are prepaid storage packages that provide discounts on storage fees. They can offset Standard, IA, and Archive storage costs. Available in 1-month, 6-month, and 1-year terms.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
