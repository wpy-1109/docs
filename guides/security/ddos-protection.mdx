OSS integrates with Anti-DDoS to defend against volumetric and application-layer attacks. When abnormal traffic is detected, OSS may place a bucket into sandbox mode to protect the platform.

## Protection levels

| Level | Description | Cost |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **Basic** | Automatic protection against common attacks. Always enabled. | Free |
| **Advanced** | Higher thresholds, traffic scrubbing, dedicated IP pools. | Paid |

## Enable advanced protection

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create an Anti-DDoS instance">
    Purchase an Anti-DDoS Pro or Premium instance from the [Anti-DDoS console](https://yundun.console.alibabacloud.com/).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Bind your bucket">
    In the OSS console, navigate to **Bucket Settings > DDoS Protection** and bind the Anti-DDoS instance to your bucket.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Sandbox mode

When OSS detects abnormal traffic patterns (traffic spikes from hotlinking, DDoS attacks, or abuse), it places the affected bucket into **sandbox mode**. In sandbox mode:

- Download speeds are throttled.
- Access may be intermittently unavailable.
- The bucket remains in sandbox until traffic returns to normal.

### Prevent sandbox activation

- Enable [hotlink protection](/guides/security/hotlink-protection) to block unauthorized embedding.
- Use [CDN acceleration](/guides/networking/cdn-acceleration) to offload traffic from OSS.
- Enable advanced DDoS protection for high-traffic buckets.
- Monitor access patterns with [access logging](/guides/logging-monitoring/access-logging).

<Warning>
If your bucket enters sandbox mode repeatedly, contact [Alibaba Cloud support](https://www.alibabacloud.com/help) to review your traffic patterns and protection configuration.
</Warning>
